Understanding Cyber Threats: Viruses, Malware, Spam, and Antiviruses


What are viruses?

Ans. Malicious programs that replicate and spread by infecting other files. They can corrupt data, steal information, or cause system malfunctions, often requiring a host program to execute.


What is malware?

Ans. Collective term for any software intended to harm or exploit, including viruses, worms, trojans, spyware, and ransomware. It targets systems, data, and users for malicious purposes like theft or damage.


What is spam?

Ans. Unsolicited bulk messages sent via email or other communication platforms. They can clutter inboxes, advertise dubious products, or serve as a vector for phishing attacks and malware distribution.


What is Anti-virus?

Ans. Software designed to detect, block, and remove malicious software. It provides real-time protection, system scanning, and regular updates to safeguard against evolving threats.
